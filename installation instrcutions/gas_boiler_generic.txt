GAS BOILER - INSTALLATION INSTRUCTIONS
PAS 2035 COMPLIANT INSTALLATION PROCEDURE

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

⚠️ CRITICAL SAFETY REQUIREMENT ⚠️

GAS BOILER INSTALLATION MUST ONLY BE CARRIED OUT BY A GAS SAFE REGISTERED ENGINEER

All work on gas appliances and associated pipework must comply with:
• Gas Safety (Installation and Use) Regulations 1998
• Building Regulations Part J (Heat producing appliances)
• Building Regulations Part L (Conservation of fuel and power)
• BS 6798 - Installation of gas-fired boilers

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. PRE-INSTALLATION REQUIREMENTS

1.1 COMPETENCY & REGISTRATION
• Installer MUST be Gas Safe registered
• Must hold appropriate competency for boiler type being installed
• Commercial Category competency required for commercial properties
• Understanding of condensing boiler technology
• Familiar with Building Regulations and gas safety requirements
• Competent in system design and heat loss calculations

1.2 SITE SURVEY & ASSESSMENT
• Conduct heat loss calculation for property
• Verify boiler size appropriate for heat demand (do not oversize)
• Assess existing heating system condition and compatibility
• Check gas supply capacity adequate for new boiler
• Verify electrical supply available (typically 3A fused spur)
• Assess flue route and termination location
• Check adequate ventilation for boiler location
• Identify condensate drainage route
• Assess water supply and pressure
• Check adequate space for boiler and clearances

1.3 GAS SUPPLY VERIFICATION
• Confirm gas meter capacity adequate for new boiler
• Check gas pipe sizing adequate for demand and run length
• Measure existing gas pressure (standing and working)
• Calculate gas pipe sizing if modifications required
• Identify gas isolation valve location

1.4 SYSTEM COMPATIBILITY CHECK
• Check existing radiators adequately sized for condensing temperatures
• Verify pipework sizing adequate
• Assess need for system cleanse and filter installation
• Check for microbore pipework (may need upgrading)
• Identify any system modifications required

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

2. PREPARATION WORKS

2.1 REMOVAL OF EXISTING BOILER (IF APPLICABLE)
• Isolate gas supply at emergency control valve
• Isolate electrical supply
• Drain heating system (or isolate boiler section)
• Disconnect gas supply at isolation valve
• Disconnect heating flow and return pipework
• Disconnect hot water pipework
• Disconnect flue system
• Remove boiler safely
• Cap off redundant flue penetration if new flue route used
• Dispose of old boiler in accordance with WEEE regulations

2.2 PREPARATION FOR NEW BOILER
• Clean and prepare wall surface for boiler mounting
• Mark boiler position using template
• Check wall is capable of supporting boiler weight
• Drill fixing holes for mounting bracket
• Install wall mounting bracket securely (use appropriate fixings for wall type)
• Check bracket is level and secure
• Plan cable and pipe routes

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

3. FLUE SYSTEM INSTALLATION

3.1 FLUE TYPE SELECTION
• Horizontal flue through external wall (most common)
• Vertical flue through roof
• Plume management kit if required (near boundaries/windows)
• Flue length and bend limitations per manufacturer's specifications

3.2 FLUE INSTALLATION REQUIREMENTS
• Use only manufacturer-approved flue components
• Maintain minimum clearances from openings (windows, doors, vents)
• Typical clearances: 300mm below openings, 300mm horizontally
• Ensure flue terminates in safe location away from:
  - Building openings (windows, doors, air vents)
  - Adjacent boundaries (minimum 600mm)
  - Public areas
  - Where plume could cause nuisance
• Maintain minimum/maximum flue lengths per manufacturer
• Support flue adequately (horizontal runs must be supported)
• Seal flue penetration through wall/roof with weatherproof seal
• Install flue with slight fall back to boiler for condensate drainage
• Fit flue terminal guard if required (public access areas)

3.3 FLUE INSTALLATION PROCEDURE
• Core drill hole through external wall (use correct diameter)
• Install flue liner through wall
• Connect flue components to boiler flue outlet
• Ensure all joints properly sealed
• Check flue is secure and correctly terminated
• Fit external terminal (maintain correct projection)
• Seal around flue penetration internally and externally
• Check flue is gas-tight (test during commissioning)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

4. BOILER INSTALLATION

4.1 MOUNTING THE BOILER
• Hang boiler on mounting bracket
• Check boiler is level and secure
• Verify adequate clearances for servicing:
  - Front: Minimum 600mm for access
  - Sides: As per manufacturer (typically 50-100mm minimum)
  - Top: As per manufacturer (typically 300mm minimum)
• Ensure boiler is securely fixed and cannot be dislodged

4.2 GAS CONNECTION
• Install gas isolation valve adjacent to boiler (if not present)
• Use rigid gas pipe or appropriate flexible connection
• Size gas pipe adequately (typically 22mm for most domestic boilers)
• Make gas connection to boiler using appropriate fittings
• Support pipework adequately
• Test gas connection for leaks (use leak detection spray or electronic detector)
• Label gas isolation valve clearly

4.3 HEATING SYSTEM CONNECTION
• Connect heating flow and return pipes to boiler
• Use appropriate fittings and ensure connections tight
• Install isolation valves on flow and return for servicing
• Ensure adequate support for pipework
• Maintain adequate clearances from electrical components

4.4 HOT WATER CONNECTION (COMBI BOILERS)
• Connect cold water inlet to boiler
• Install isolation valve on cold supply
• Connect hot water outlet from boiler
• Ensure adequate pipe sizing (typically 22mm)
• Check cold water pressure adequate (typically 1-4 bar)
• Install pressure reducing valve if mains pressure exceeds boiler maximum

4.5 CONDENSATE DRAINAGE
• Connect condensate pipe to boiler condensate outlet
• Route condensate to appropriate drainage point:
  - Internal foul drain (preferred)
  - External drain
  - Soakaway (only if no other option)
• Use minimum 21.5mm (3/4") pipe diameter internally
• Increase to 32mm if any external runs
• Ensure pipe has continuous fall (minimum 3° angle)
• Install condensate trap if required by manufacturer
• Protect external condensate pipe from freezing:
  - Insulate pipe adequately
  - Increase diameter to 32mm minimum
  - Keep runs as short as possible
  - Consider trace heating in severe frost areas

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

5. ELECTRICAL INSTALLATION

5.1 ELECTRICAL CONNECTION
• Install 3A fused spur adjacent to boiler
• Wire permanent live, switched live, neutral, and earth
• Use appropriate cable size (typically 1.0mm² or 1.5mm²)
• Connect to boiler following manufacturer's wiring diagram
• Ensure RCD protection provided
• Test electrical connections and verify correct voltage
• All electrical work must comply with Part P Building Regulations

5.2 CONTROLS INSTALLATION
• Install room thermostat in appropriate location:
  - Main living area (typically living room or hallway)
  - Away from heat sources, draughts, and direct sunlight
  - At height of approximately 1.5m
  - Not in kitchen or bathroom
• Install programmer/timer for heating schedules
• Wire controls to boiler following manufacturer's wiring diagram
• Consider weather compensation controls for improved efficiency
• Install cylinder thermostat if system boiler with separate cylinder

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

6. SYSTEM PREPARATION

6.1 SYSTEM CLEANSING (ESSENTIAL)
• Flush heating system thoroughly before connecting new boiler
• Use proprietary system cleaner for heavily contaminated systems
• Circulate cleaner through system as per instructions
• Flush with clean water until runs clear
• Install magnetic system filter (essential for warranty)
• Install filter on heating return before boiler

6.2 SYSTEM FILLING
• Close all drain valves
• Open all radiator valves
• Fill system slowly via filling loop
• Vent all radiators starting from lowest
• Continue filling to correct pressure (typically 1.0-1.5 bar cold)
• Add system inhibitor (dose as per manufacturer)
• Ensure inhibitor compatible with boiler manufacturer's warranty requirements
• Record inhibitor product and dose rate

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

7. COMMISSIONING

7.1 PRE-COMMISSIONING CHECKS
• Verify all installation work completed correctly
• Check all isolation valves open
• Verify gas supply connected and valve open
• Check electrical supply connected and energized
• Confirm flue correctly installed and sealed
• Verify condensate drainage connected
• Check system filled and pressurized
• Ensure all packaging and shipping brackets removed from boiler

7.2 GAS SOUNDNESS TESTING
• Perform gas tightness test on all new/modified gas pipework
• Test at working pressure for minimum 2 minutes
• Use manometer or electronic pressure tester
• Check for any pressure drop (zero pressure drop acceptable)
• Identify and rectify any leaks before proceeding

7.3 INITIAL BOILER START-UP
• Turn on electrical supply
• Turn on gas supply
• Power up boiler
• Follow manufacturer's commissioning procedure
• Monitor first ignition and burner operation
• Check for smooth operation and no unusual noises
• Verify flame picture correct (view through inspection window if provided)
• Check flue gases evacuating correctly

7.4 GAS RATE VERIFICATION
• Measure gas consumption using meter timing method
• Calculate gas rate (m³/hr or ft³/hr)
• Verify gas rate matches manufacturer's specification
• Adjust if required following manufacturer's procedure
• Typically within ±5% of rated input

7.5 SYSTEM COMMISSIONING
• Set boiler parameters:
  - Maximum flow temperature (typically 75°C for existing radiators, 50-55°C for condensing mode)
  - Hot water temperature (typically 60°C)
  - Pump speed (adjust for system resistance)
• Balance heating system:
  - Lockshield radiators to achieve equal temperature drop across all radiators
  - Typical design temperature drop: 20°C (80°C flow, 60°C return for high temp, or 50°C flow, 30°C return for low temp)
• Test hot water production (combi boilers)
• Check system pressure under operating conditions
• Verify no leaks throughout system

7.6 COMBUSTION ANALYSIS
• Conduct flue gas analysis using calibrated analyzer
• Measure:
  - Oxygen (O2) levels
  - Carbon dioxide (CO2) levels  
  - Carbon monoxide (CO) levels
  - Flue gas temperature
  - Combustion efficiency
• Record readings on commissioning sheet
• Verify readings within manufacturer's acceptable range:
  - CO level: Maximum 200ppm (preferably <50ppm)
  - CO2: Typically 8-10% (gas boilers)
  - Efficiency: >85% (condensing boilers should exceed 90%)
• Adjust if required and re-test

7.7 SAFETY DEVICE TESTING
• Test flame failure device (FSD)
• Test overheat thermostat operation
• Test pressure relief valve (if fitted)
• Verify low water pressure cut-out operates
• Test all interlocks and safety systems

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

8. CONTROLS COMMISSIONING

8.1 HEATING CONTROLS SETUP
• Program heating schedule to customer requirements
• Set room temperature set point (typically 21°C)
• Configure setback temperatures for night/away (typically 16-18°C)
• Test thermostat calls for heat correctly
• Verify boiler responds to control signals
• Set TRV positions on individual radiators

8.2 HOT WATER CONTROLS (SYSTEM BOILERS)
• Set cylinder thermostat to 60°C (legionella prevention)
• Test hot water zone operates independently
• Verify priority hot water operation if configured

8.3 ADVANCED CONTROLS (IF FITTED)
• Configure weather compensation curve
• Set up zoning controls if multi-zone system
• Program smart controls and connect to WiFi if applicable
• Test all control modes and functions

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

9. FINAL CHECKS & TESTING

9.1 OPERATIONAL TESTING
• Run boiler through complete heating cycle
• Verify smooth modulation and cycling
• Check all radiators heat up correctly
• Test hot water production (flow rate and temperature)
• Monitor system pressure remains stable
• Check for any leaks throughout system
• Verify condensate draining correctly
• Confirm no unusual noises or vibration

9.2 VENTILATION CHECK
• Verify adequate ventilation for boiler location (if required)
• Check air vents unobstructed
• Measure room ventilation if boiler in confined space
• Ensure combustion air supply adequate

9.3 DOCUMENTATION
• Complete manufacturer's commissioning checklist
• Record all settings and parameters
• Complete Gas Safe benchmark checklist
• Photograph installation and gas meter reading
• Record serial numbers (boiler, controls, filter)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

10. CUSTOMER HANDOVER

10.1 SYSTEM DEMONSTRATION
• Demonstrate boiler operation and controls
• Show how to adjust room temperature
• Explain heating programmer/timer operation
• Demonstrate hot water controls (if applicable)
• Show how to check system pressure
• Demonstrate how to re-pressurize system using filling loop
• Show location of isolation valves and emergency controls
• Explain boiler status indicators and error codes

10.2 IMPORTANT CUSTOMER ADVICE
• Annual service essential (explain importance)
• System filter requires periodic cleaning (typically 12 months)
• Maintain system pressure between 1.0-1.5 bar
• Never adjust gas valve or internal settings
• Report any unusual smells, noises, or operation immediately
• Explain condensing boiler operation (normal to see plume)
• Advise on optimal temperature settings for efficiency
• Provide emergency contact details

10.3 DOCUMENTATION PROVIDED
• Manufacturer's user manual
• Warranty registration details
• Gas Safe installation certificate (CP12 if rental)
• Building Regulations compliance certificate
• Commissioning records and test results
• Emergency contact details
• Maintenance schedule
• Filter cleaning instructions

10.4 WARRANTY REGISTRATION
• Register boiler warranty with manufacturer immediately
• Provide customer with warranty certificate
• Explain warranty terms and annual service requirements
• Register system filter to maintain boiler warranty

11. BUILDING CONTROL NOTIFICATION

11.1 BUILDING REGULATIONS COMPLIANCE
• Notify Building Control of installation (if required)
• Complete Competent Person Scheme notification OR
• Apply for Building Regulations approval
• Provide compliance certificate to customer
• File copy of notification/approval for records

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

12. MAINTENANCE REQUIREMENTS

12.1 ANNUAL SERVICE (MANDATORY)
Essential for warranty and safety:
• Visual inspection of boiler and flue system
• Combustion analysis and burner adjustment
• Clean burner and heat exchanger if required
• Check and clean ignition system
• Test all safety devices
• Check gas rate and pressure
• Inspect and test flue system
• Check condensate drainage
• Clean or replace system filter
• Inspect electrical connections
• Check system pressure and inhibitor concentration
• Test controls operation
• Provide service report to customer

12.2 PERIODIC CHECKS (CUSTOMER)
• Check system pressure monthly (top up if below 1.0 bar)
• Monitor boiler operation for any changes
• Check for any leaks around boiler or pipework
• Ensure adequate ventilation maintained
• Keep boiler area clear of storage/obstructions

14. CRITICAL SAFETY WARNINGS

⚠️ GAS SAFE REGISTRATION: Only Gas Safe registered engineers may work on gas appliances
⚠️ CARBON MONOXIDE: Poorly installed/maintained boilers can produce CO - fit CO alarm
⚠️ VENTILATION: Adequate ventilation essential - never block air vents
⚠️ FLUE: Regular flue checks essential - blockage can cause CO production
⚠️ COMBUSTION AIR: Ensure adequate air supply for combustion
⚠️ ANNUAL SERVICE: Essential for safety and efficiency - not optional
⚠️ MODIFICATIONS: Never modify gas controls or settings
⚠️ SMELL GAS: If gas smell detected - turn off supply, ventilate, call National Gas Emergency: 0800 111 999
