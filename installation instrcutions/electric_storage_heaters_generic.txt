ELECTRIC STORAGE HEATERS - INSTALLATION INSTRUCTIONS
PAS 2035 COMPLIANT INSTALLATION PROCEDURE

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. PRE-INSTALLATION REQUIREMENTS

1.1 COMPETENCY & QUALIFICATIONS
• Installer must be qualified electrician (Part P registered)
• Competent to work with high heat retention storage heater systems
• Understanding of Economy 7/10 tariff systems
• Familiar with manufacturer's installation requirements
• Public Liability Insurance in place

1.2 SITE SURVEY & ASSESSMENT
• Confirm heat loss calculations completed for each room
• Verify heater sizes appropriate for room dimensions
• Check electrical supply capacity adequate for total load
• Verify Economy 7 or similar off-peak tariff available
• Assess wall construction for mounting suitability
• Identify existing heater positions and wiring
• Check for adequate clearances around proposed locations
• Confirm smart meter installed if required for tariff

1.3 CUSTOMER CONSULTATION
• Explain storage heater operation and charging times
• Discuss Economy 7/10 tariff requirements and timing
• Advise on input/output controls and settings
• Explain smart controls and programming if applicable
• Discuss expected running costs vs existing system
• Provide written quotation including all costs

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

2. REMOVAL OF EXISTING HEATERS (IF APPLICABLE)

2.1 SAFE REMOVAL PROCEDURE
• Isolate electrical supply at consumer unit
• Verify isolation with voltage tester
• Label circuits clearly
• Disconnect heater from electrical supply
• Remove heater carefully (storage heaters are heavy - typically 50-150kg)
• Use appropriate manual handling equipment
• Dispose of old heater in accordance with WEEE regulations
• Check condition of existing wiring and junction boxes

2.2 WALL PREPARATION
• Make good any wall damage from old heater removal
• Check wall is sound, dry, and capable of supporting new heater weight
• Repair any cracks or damage
• Ensure wall is level and plumb

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

3. HEATER SIZING & POSITIONING

3.1 HEATER SELECTION
• Select heater capacity based on room heat loss calculation
• Typical sizing: 0.5-1.5kW per room depending on insulation and room size
• Consider room usage patterns and heating requirements
• Ensure heater output matches heat demand
• Select appropriate control type (manual, programmable, or smart)

3.2 POSITIONING REQUIREMENTS
• Install under windows where possible (prevents cold downdrafts)
• Maintain minimum clearances:
  - 50mm from floor (or as per manufacturer specification)
  - 50mm from walls/corners
  - 100mm from curtains/furniture
  - 300mm from ceiling
  - 500mm in front for air circulation
• Avoid positioning behind doors or furniture
• Ensure adequate air circulation around heater
• Position away from thermostats or temperature sensors
• Consider room layout and furniture placement

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

4. ELECTRICAL INSTALLATION

4.1 ELECTRICAL SUPPLY REQUIREMENTS
• Each heater requires dedicated radial circuit
• Circuit protection: MCB rated appropriately for heater load
• Cable sizing: Minimum 2.5mm² (check manufacturer and regulations)
• Install RCD protection (30mA)
• Heaters must be connected to off-peak supply for charging
• Some models require both off-peak (charging) and continuous (fan) supplies

4.2 WIRING CONFIGURATION
Standard configurations:
• OFF-PEAK ONLY: Single feed from Economy 7 supply for basic heaters
• DUAL SUPPLY: Off-peak for charging + continuous for fan/boost (High Heat Retention models)
• SMART CONTROL: May require additional control cable for wireless/smart controls

4.3 CIRCUIT INSTALLATION
• Run cable from consumer unit to heater location
• Use appropriate cable route (surface, buried, or concealed)
• Install junction box or connection unit near heater
• Ensure all connections comply with BS 7671 wiring regulations
• Use heat-resistant cable at heater connection point
• Fit cable correctly into heater terminals (not under strain)
• Terminate cables securely with no exposed conductors

4.4 OFF-PEAK SUPPLY CONNECTION
• Connect heater charging circuit to Economy 7 supply
• Verify correct tariff meter and supply
• Ensure off-peak timing matches energy supplier settings
• Label circuits clearly: "Storage Heater - Off Peak Supply"
• Test supply timing with supplier if required

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

5. HEATER MOUNTING & INSTALLATION

5.1 WALL MOUNTING (TYPICAL METHOD)
• Mark heater position on wall using spirit level
• Mark fixing points using heater template or measurements
• Drill fixing holes using appropriate drill and bit for wall type
• Insert wall plugs suitable for wall construction and heater weight
• Position mounting brackets and secure with appropriate fixings
• Verify brackets are level and secure
• Lift heater onto brackets (use two people - heaters are heavy)
• Secure heater to brackets using manufacturer's fixings
• Check heater is level and stable

5.2 FLOOR-STANDING MODELS
• Position heater on floor in marked location
• Ensure heater is stable and level
• Some models may require wall brackets for stability
• Maintain clearances as specified
• Ensure heater cannot be easily moved or tipped

5.3 ELECTRICAL CONNECTION
• Connect electrical supply to heater terminals as per manufacturer's wiring diagram
• Typical connections:
  - L (Live) - Brown
  - N (Neutral) - Blue
  - E (Earth) - Green/Yellow
• For dual-supply heaters, connect both charging and continuous supplies correctly
• Ensure all connections are tight and secure
• Fit any cable clamps or strain relief as required
• Refit heater cover/panels

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

6. CONTROL INSTALLATION

6.1 INTEGRAL CONTROLS
• Set input charge control (controls how much heat is stored)
• Set output control (controls heat release rate)
• Some models have automatic charge control linked to room thermostat
• Set room temperature target if programmable

6.2 PROGRAMMABLE/SMART CONTROLS
• Install room sensor in appropriate location (away from heater, draughts, direct sunlight)
• Pair wireless controls with heaters following manufacturer's procedure
• Configure heating schedule and temperatures
• Set Economy 7 timing in control system
• Test wireless communication between control and heater
• Provide user with control instructions and login details if cloud-connected

6.3 THERMOSTAT SETTINGS
• Set appropriate temperature for each room (typically 18-21°C)
• Configure setback temperatures for night/away periods
• Program heating schedule if applicable
• Enable smart features such as open window detection if available

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

7. COMMISSIONING & TESTING

7.1 ELECTRICAL TESTING
• Conduct full electrical installation test:
  - Continuity of protective conductors
  - Insulation resistance test (minimum 1MΩ)
  - Polarity check
  - Earth fault loop impedance
  - RCD operation test
• Verify supply voltage correct
• Check off-peak supply timing
• Measure current draw under load

7.2 HEATER FUNCTION TESTING
• Switch on electrical supply at consumer unit
• Verify heater receives power (indicator light/display)
• Test all controls and functions
• For off-peak: May need to wait for charging period or use manual boost
• Check fan operation if fitted (High Heat Retention models)
• Verify thermostatic control operates correctly
• Test safety cutouts (overheat protection)
• Check heater heats up during off-peak period
• Verify output control functions correctly

7.3 SAFETY CHECKS
• Verify all covers and panels securely fitted
• Check no exposed live parts
• Ensure heater stable and secure on wall/floor
• Verify adequate clearances maintained
• Check cables not subject to heat damage
• Ensure earth bonding correct

8. CUSTOMER HANDOVER

8.1 DEMONSTRATION & INSTRUCTION
• Demonstrate how to set input charge control
• Explain output control operation
• Show how to use manual boost if fitted
• Demonstrate programmable controls and scheduling
• Explain Economy 7 charging times
• Advise on optimal settings for different weather conditions
• Show how to adjust room temperature
• Demonstrate smart control app if applicable

8.2 DOCUMENTATION
• Provide manufacturer's user manual
• Supply warranty information and registration details
• Issue electrical installation certificate (Part P)
• Provide emergency contact details
• Give maintenance schedule and requirements
• Issue insurance-backed guarantee

8.3 CUSTOMER ADVICE
• Explain heating patterns differ from gas central heating (constant background heat)
• Advise on pre-heating rooms before very cold weather
• Explain importance of correct charge settings (avoid overcharging)
• Recommend not covering heaters or restricting airflow
• Advise annual inspection recommended
• Explain Economy 7 tariff optimization
• Provide guidance on expected running costs

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

9. MAINTENANCE & SERVICING

9.1 ANNUAL CHECKS (RECOMMENDED)
• Visual inspection of heaters for damage
• Check electrical connections remain tight
• Clean dust/debris from heater vents and grilles
• Verify controls operate correctly
• Check room temperatures achieve set points
• Inspect cables for any heat damage
• Verify no furniture blocking heaters
• Test RCD operation

9.2 USER MAINTENANCE
• Clean exterior surfaces with damp cloth
• Vacuum grilles periodically to remove dust
• Check and adjust charge settings seasonally
• Monitor electricity usage and costs
• Report any unusual smells or noises immediately

11. IMPORTANT SAFETY NOTES

• WEIGHT: Storage heaters are extremely heavy (50-150kg) - use proper manual handling
• HEAT: Core temperatures exceed 600°C - never open heater when charged
• ASBESTOS: Some older storage heaters contain asbestos - specialist removal required
• ELECTRICAL: All work must be Part P compliant - issue certificate
• CLEARANCES: Must maintain minimum clearances to prevent fire risk
• VENTILATION: Never block air vents or grilles
• COVERS: Never dry clothes or place items on storage heaters
• CHILDREN: Consider safety guards if young children present
• ECONOMY 7: Verify tariff active before installation - essential for operation
